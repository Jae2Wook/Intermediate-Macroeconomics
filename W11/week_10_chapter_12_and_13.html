(c) Economists refer to the result in the IS-MPR diagram as crowding out. What gets crowded out and why?

Crowding out is a situation that the interest rate gets high because there is no much of money left in banks to lend. MPR: Rt = n*Y_st + r. As a government lends more money, aggregate demand shock increases becuase the government purchases increases. Then, we can see that the real interest rate in MPR increases naturally. So, MPR can be as a indicator for crowding out.

Q4) The Taylor rule:¶

John Taylor of Stanford University proposed the following monetary policy rule: $$ R_t - \bar{r} = \bar{m}(\pi_{t}-\bar{\pi})+\bar{n}\tilde{Y}_{t} $$

That is, Taylor suggests that monetary policy should increase the real interest rate whenever output exceeds potential.

a) What is the economic justification for such a rule?

r = 2, m = 0.5, inf_s = 2

b) Combine this policy rule with the IS curve to get a new aggregate demand curve. How does it differ from the AD curve we considered in the chapter? Consider the response of short-run output to aggregate demand shocks and inflation shocks

AD : Ys = (a / (1+bn)) - (bm / (1 + bn)) * (inf - inf_s)

aggregate demand shocks will impact less than the original AD curve because 1 + bn > 1. So, the curve will shift less out.

inflation shocks will impact less than the original AD curve because 1 + bn > 1, So, the curve will shift less slope change.

c) Redo Q3) using this new AD curve. Assume that $\bar{n}=1/2$. Please describe the effect of a different monetary policy rule on business cycles.
